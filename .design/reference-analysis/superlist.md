# Superlist Reference Analysis

Sources: https://www.superlist.com, https://www.superlist.com/feature-lists, https://www.superlist.com/pricing

## Layout
- Playful productivity layout with large friendly blocks, expressive spacing, and approachable product screenshots.
- Surfaces feel personal and team-oriented, balancing utility with delight.

## Navigation
- Simple top-level marketing navigation; product UI uses list/task hierarchy and shared spaces.

## Typography
- Friendly, bold headlines with readable body copy.
- Uses bigger, warmer hierarchy than Linear.

## Grid
- Alternates editorial sections with product screenshots and feature blocks.
- Cards can be large, colorful, and asymmetrical.

## Components
- Task rows, notes, list blocks, collaboration indicators, pricing cards, AI feature blocks.

## Cards
- Cards are more expressive: rounded, colorful, illustration-friendly, and emotionally warmer.

## Hero
- Clear value statement with product screenshot and direct free-signup CTA.

## Motion
- Motion should feel delightful but not gimmicky: quick transitions, soft reveals, responsive card states.

## CTA
- Simple, friendly, repeated CTA language.

## Color
- More playful contrast than Linear; warm accents and bright product blocks.

## Icons
- Simple icons that feel friendly and lightweight.

## Empty State
- Empty states can be warmer, more encouraging, and action-oriented.

## Loading
- Use skeletons/progress that feel calm and optimistic.

## Visual Philosophy
- Beautiful work/life productivity: useful, friendly, polished, and not overloaded.
