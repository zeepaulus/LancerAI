# Notion Reference Analysis

Sources: https://www.notion.com, https://www.notion.com/help/intro-to-workspaces, https://www.notion.com/help/navigate-with-the-sidebar

## Layout
- Workspace-first hierarchy: pages, blocks, databases, and nested information.
- Readability and organization matter more than decoration.

## Navigation
- Sidebar structures workspace content, pages, shared spaces, and personal areas.

## Typography
- Calm document typography with clear headings and readable line length.

## Grid
- Flexible page/block system; databases and lists adapt to content.

## Components
- Pages, blocks, databases, properties, breadcrumbs, page icons, sidebars.

## Cards
- Cards should support organization and readability, not fight the content.

## Hero
- Product promise is workspace clarity and AI assistance.

## Motion
- Gentle and low-distraction.

## CTA
- Start organizing, create workspace, ask AI.

## Color
- Mostly neutral, document-like, with occasional soft color.

## Icons
- Small page icons and lightweight affordances.

## Empty State
- Empty pages should invite creation and provide templates.

## Loading
- Keep page shape stable; skeletons should match content blocks.

## Visual Philosophy
- Calm knowledge workspace with nested clarity and low cognitive load.
