# Stripe Reference Analysis

Sources: https://stripe.com, https://pages.charlimarie.com/posts/what-we-can-learn-from-this-new-stripe-landing-page

## Layout
- Editorial landing composition with layered product storytelling and strong section pacing.
- Large hero with product value, proof, and rich visual atmosphere.

## Navigation
- Top navigation groups product families and conversion paths.

## Typography
- Confident headlines, clear body rhythm, and compact supporting labels.

## Grid
- Uses editorial grids, overlapping visuals, and responsive product modules.

## Components
- Hero scenes, product panels, CTA groups, customer proof, feature blocks, data snippets.

## Cards
- Cards may overlap or float within large visual systems, but must remain readable.

## Hero
- Strongest source for LancerAI landing: layered gradients, product promise, and visual storytelling.

## Motion
- Motion should feel fluid and premium, especially in hero and transitions.

## CTA
- Primary CTA is confident; secondary CTA supports exploration.

## Color
- Rich gradients with disciplined typography and high contrast.

## Icons
- Icons support product categories; they should be polished and not playful for their own sake.

## Empty State
- Not a core Stripe pattern for app UI, but landing copy teaches next step clearly.

## Loading
- Use polished progressive disclosure for rich visuals.

## Visual Philosophy
- Complex product made desirable through beautiful, layered storytelling.
